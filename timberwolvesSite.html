Timberwolves Make the Western Conference Championship

The Minnesota Timberwolves reached the Western Conference finals brimming with talent, trust and tenacity after staging the biggest Game 7 comeback since the NBA began tracking play-by-play data 28 years ago.

The Wolves trailed the Denver Nuggets by 15 points at halftime Sunday night and by 20 points just over a minute into the third quarter.

With the crowd rocking Ball Arena, the Timberwolves didn’t flinch. They doubled down on their dogged defense and roared back for the most monumental victory in the franchise’s 35-year history.

They did it behind a terrific transition game and an unwavering superstar in Anthony Edwards for a stunning 98-90 victory over the reigning NBA champions.

The Wolves will face the Dallas Mavericks beginning Wednesday night at Target Center.

Their Superstar Anthony Edwards

Minnesota Timberwolves star guard Anthony Edwards created a viral moment when he told TNT's Charles Barkley to "bring ya ass" to Minnesota for the Western Conference Finals.

Now, Edwards' one-liner is being used to attract visitors to the state, as BringYaAss.com has been established as a website that redirects to the official Minnesota tourism website.

The Timberwolves had the highest defensive rating in the league this season, showcasing their tenacity and teamwork.

Anthony Edwards set a new franchise record for most points scored in a single season, solidifying his status as a rising star.

The Timberwolves achieved their longest winning streak in franchise history this season, with 12 consecutive victories.
